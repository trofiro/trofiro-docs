A live meeting is a secure appointment with your patient — over video when you meet remotely, or audio-only when you're together in person. This guide covers hosting, joining as a patient, seeing the patient chart and every in-call control.

## Roles in a meeting

| Role | What they can do |
| --- | --- |
| **Practitioner** | Starts the meeting room, invites the patient, keeps the patient chart at hand, and ends the call for everyone. |
| **Patient** | Joins through an invite link without an account, takes part with video, audio, chat, and screen sharing, and can leave at any time. |

## How meeting access works

### Your meeting room

As a practitioner, you sign in to Trofiro and open your meeting room address — it looks like `…/live/your-room-name`. The meeting starts the moment the room opens.

### Patient invite links

Your patient joins through a secure invite link that you copy from inside the meeting and send to them. The link opens the join screen in their browser — no sign-in needed.

<Warning>
  Invite links expire after a set period. An expired or altered link shows "**Invalid or expired link**". Send the link close to the appointment time, and send a fresh one if in doubt.
</Warning>

## Host a live meeting

<Warning>
  The room goes live as soon as you open it — your camera and microphone are **on** immediately, and there is no preview screen. Be ready before you open the room.
</Warning>

<Steps>

  <Step title="Open your meeting room">
    Sign in to Trofiro and open your meeting room address in the browser.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Copy the invite link">
    While you're alone in the room, the waiting card offers a **Copy link** button. The same link is always available from the link button in the control bar.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Send the link to your patient">
    Use any channel you already use with your patient — email, messaging, or your practice's usual workflow.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for your patient to join">
    The card shows "**Waiting for {name} to join…**" with the appointment time, and you see your own camera. When your patient connects, the live view starts automatically.
  </Step>

</Steps>

### Your controls during the appointment

| Control | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Microphone** | Mute or unmute yourself. Pick a different microphone with the device picker next to the toggle (desktop). |
| **Camera** | Turn your video on or off. Pick a different camera with the device picker (desktop). |
| **Screen share** | Share your screen with your patient (desktop only). |
| **Chat** | Open the text chat panel. A badge shows unread messages while the panel is closed. |
| **Pin** | Hover over any tile and pin it to keep it in the main view. |
| **Copy invite link** | Copy your patient's invite link again at any point in the call. |
| **Patient chart** | Show or hide the patient chart panel. |
| End-call button (red phone icon) | End the meeting for everyone. |

### Use the patient chart

The patient chart opens by default at the side of the call, so your patient's information stays in view while you talk. Its **Recommendations** section is where dietary and treatment recommendations will appear — until then it shows "**No recommendations yet**".

On a phone, the chart overlays the video instead of docking beside it, and stays out of the way until you open it.

### Share your screen

Select the screen share control (desktop only) and choose what to share. Your shared screen becomes the main tile for everyone automatically — your tile is labeled "**Your screen**", and your patient sees it as "**{name}'s screen**". Select the control again to stop sharing.

### End the call

Select the red end-call button to finish the appointment for everyone. A loading page appears for a couple of seconds, then you're taken to your home page automatically.

<Note>
  Your patient can leave on their own at any time — that doesn't end the meeting for you. Only you can end it for everyone.
</Note>

## Join a live meeting as a patient

Everything you need is the invite link from your practitioner. The meeting runs in your browser — nothing to install, no account, no password.

<Steps>

  <Step title="Open your invite link">
    The "**Ready to join?**" screen appears. Check your camera and microphone, add your name, and enter when you're ready.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check your camera">
    The preview tile shows exactly what your practitioner will see, marked with a "**Preview**" badge. Use the camera toggle to turn video on or off — when it's off, the screen shows "**Your camera is off**". Use the picker to switch cameras.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check your microphone">
    Use the microphone toggle and picker the same way. Camera and microphone both start on.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm your appointment">
    The appointment card shows who you're meeting and the time.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter your name">
    Type your name in the **Your name** field — it's how you'll appear in the call. This is required: if it's empty, you'll see "**Name is required**".
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Join room">
    You enter the meeting with the camera and microphone choices you made on the join screen.
  </Step>

</Steps>

### Your controls

| Control | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Microphone** | Mute or unmute yourself. Pick a different microphone with the device picker (desktop). |
| **Camera** | Turn your video on or off. Pick a different camera with the device picker (desktop). |
| **Screen share** | Share your screen with your practitioner (desktop only). |
| **Chat** | Open the text chat. A badge shows unread messages while it's closed. |
| **Pin** | Hover over any tile and pin it to keep it in the main view. |
| End-call button (red phone icon) | Leave the meeting. The meeting continues for your practitioner. |

### What you see on screen

The person speaking fills the main tile, with everyone else in thumbnails. When someone shares their screen, it takes the main view automatically. Pin any tile to keep it in the main view regardless of who's talking. A colored ring highlights whoever is speaking, and each tile shows a name and microphone status. On a phone, your own camera floats as a small tile over the call.

### Leave the meeting

Select the red end-call button when you're done. You'll see "**Your call has ended**" — and "You can safely close this window."

<Tip>
  Left by accident? Open your invite link again to rejoin, as long as it hasn't expired.
</Tip>

<Note>
  If your practitioner ends the call, the meeting ends for everyone at the same time.
</Note>

## Controls and layout reference

The full picture of every in-call control, who has it, and where it works:

| Control | What it does | Available to | Platforms |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Microphone | Mute or unmute | Both | Desktop and mobile |
| Camera | Video on or off | Both | Desktop and mobile |
| Microphone picker | Switch microphone | Both | Desktop |
| Camera picker | Switch camera | Both | Desktop |
| Screen share | Share your screen | Both | Desktop |
| Chat | Text chat panel | Both | Desktop and mobile |
| Pin | Keep a tile in the main view | Both | Desktop and mobile |
| Copy invite link | Copy the patient's link | Practitioner | Desktop and mobile |
| Patient chart | Show or hide the chart panel | Practitioner | Desktop and mobile |
| End-call button (practitioner) | Ends the meeting for everyone | Practitioner | Desktop and mobile |
| End-call button (patient) | Leaves the meeting; it continues for others | Patient | Desktop and mobile |

### Chat

- Press **Enter** to send, **Shift+Enter** for a new line.
- Messages can be up to 750 characters. Long messages collapse in the thread with a **Show more** option.
- While the panel is closed, a badge on the chat button counts unread messages.
- The thread also notes arrivals and departures: "**{name} joined the call**", "**{name} left the call**".
- On a phone, the chat panel overlays the video instead of docking beside it.

### Video layout

- The layout is automatic: one main tile plus thumbnails.
- The main tile follows this priority: a **pinned** tile first, then a **shared screen**, then whoever is **speaking**.
- Hover over any tile to reveal the pin button. Pinning overrides the automatic focus; unpin to hand control back.
- A colored ring marks the active speaker, and every tile overlays a name and microphone status.
- While you're alone in the room, you see your own camera as a full self-view.
- On a phone, your self-view floats as a small picture-in-picture tile.

### Desktop and mobile differences

| Capability | Desktop | Mobile |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Screen share | Available | Not available |
| Device pickers | Available | Uses your system defaults |
| Chat and patient chart | Dock beside the video | Overlay the video |
| Self-view | Part of the layout | Floating picture-in-picture tile |

## Audio-only mode for in-person appointments

When your patient is sitting across from you, video adds nothing — audio-only mode turns a live meeting into a lightweight companion for in-person appointments. The screen shows your avatar with a live microphone waveform and the label "**In-person appointment · live audio**", so you can confirm at a glance that your microphone is picking up the conversation.

<Info>
  Audio-only mode is for practitioners. Patients don't join these sessions — they're in the room with you.
</Info>

<Steps>

  <Step title="Open your meeting room in audio mode">
    Add `?mode=audio` to your meeting room address — for example `…/live/your-room-name?mode=audio`.

    <Warning>
      Your microphone is live the moment the session opens. Use the microphone control to mute whenever you need to.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run the appointment">
    Speak naturally — the waveform moves while your microphone picks up sound. The patient chart is available at the side, just like in a video meeting.
  </Step>

  <Step title="End the session">
    Select the red end-call button. A loading page appears for a couple of seconds, then you return to your home page automatically.
  </Step>

</Steps>

Compared with a video meeting, audio-only mode has:

- No camera and no video tiles — just your avatar and the waveform
- No screen sharing
- No chat panel
- No invite link — there's no one to invite
